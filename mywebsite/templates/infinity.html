Multidimensional Infinity Mirror

  • Company/Group: Personal Project

After using mirror film for much of Summer 2018 and finding myself with plenty of it spare, I wanted to see what else I could do with it. I came across a short video of a dodecahedron infinity mirror and was amazed by its beauty and deep-space feeling. Without an explanation of how it was designed, I decided to try and make it myself using the spare mirror film that I had available. Â 

  • Thickness of clear acrylic available to me
  • Physical dimensions of the LED strips
  • Power requirements to run the electronics safely
  • Mechanical connections needed to hold the shape together and allow for it to be carried

I began by calculating the required angles to form a dodecahedron, and designed it in Solidworks parametrically so that I could vary the edge length. By calculating the number of total LEDs that would be needed given the total edge length and the LEDs per metre, I also found the total current and thus power supply required to run the LEDs. I 3D printed the sides and connectors, laser cut the hexagon faces and applied mirror film to each face.
