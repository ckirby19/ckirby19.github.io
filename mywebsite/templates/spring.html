SPRING

  • Company/Group: MIT Media Lab, Affective Computing
  • My Role: Electrical Engineer and Research Assistant
  • Dates: January 2017-June 2017
  • Further Information: Project page on MIT Media Lab website here

The Smart Platform for Research, Intervention and Neurodevelopmental Growth (SPRING) is a a smart block placing toy for children with Neurodevelopmental disorders. Designed to stimulate the child's cognitive, social and motor development, whilst automatically collecting data on their progression in order to enable better research. 

SPRING collects data by triggering a reed switch whenever the blocks are placed accurately into the hole. In order to collect new data on what the child does before accurately placing the blocks, explore methods to track the child's movement of the block around the toy. Do they place the block straight away, or do they move the block around the top face before finding where to place it?

  • Detect scraping and hitting of block pieces against the toys top surface
  • Fit inside the current toy design with minimum adjustments
  • Be placed out of sight from the child
  • Durability against being thrown and kicked around

I began by prototyping an array of hall effect sensors, measuring the magnetic field strength generated by the magnet in each block in order to locate them. The hall effect sensors could also be positioned to replace the reed switches.

The Piezo microphone was tested inside of the toy and was found to give accurate information about a users hand movement on the wooden surface. Unfortunately I did not stay on the team long enough to see if the device was tested in a real setting.
