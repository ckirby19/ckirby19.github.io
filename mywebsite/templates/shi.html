Wall Climbing Robot

  • Company/Group: Sumitomo Heavy Industries Robotics Group, Japan
  • My Role: Mechanical Engineer
  • Dates: June 2019 - June 2020

Â As part of the SHI Robotics Group, a project on a wall climbing robot was being developed. Subject to NDA, further details cannot be revealed.

The current design made it difficult for the robot to navigate on curved surfaces. A redesign of the power transmission system is needed to improve the slope angle limit. At the same time, a simulation of the robot would be useful for testing of line following algorithms for autonomous control of the robot on a surface.

Four initial power transmission systems were designed on Solidworks and compared using a Pugh table, with a focus especially on the width reduction that the power transmission would enable. After deciding upon a design, I then fully developed it and calculated the required gear ratios to maintain the same RPM needed for the robot to complete the same tasks as before. I constructed and conducted early performance tests on the transmission system and testing continues to occur to refine it. For the simulation, I began by researching other robot simulations in Unity and recreating the real robot body in a simplified form in Blender. After better understanding the Physics engine used in Unity, I designed the test environment and robot for useful simulations to be created. Some of the simulations included:

  • Classical PID controller testing on a flat and bumpy surface
  • Line following using a basic computer vision system
  • Surface adaptive PID controller testing and comparison with a classical PID controller
  • Wall climbing transitions and line following between transitions

A 30% increase in the angle limit of the slope the robot is able to climb, and a test environment was created in Unity for further research into the best control scheme for autonomous control of the robot on various surfaces.
